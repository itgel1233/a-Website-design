Problems were facing

Even on more typical winter days, outdoor and indoor PM2.5 in ger districts often exceeds Mongolia’s national standard by more than ten times.

Traffic congestion is a major contributor — diesel cars and old engines emit a lot of nitrogen oxides.

On many days, especially in winter, air quality becomes “unhealthy for all” or “very unhealthy” for sensitive groups, with AQI (Air Quality Index) values much higher than safe thresholds.

UB’s air pollution causes children to develop asthma, bronchitis, and other lung problems. Long-term exposure can stunt lung growth and make them more prone to illness.

Parents worry constantly about their children’s health during the smog season. This stress affects daily life, limits outdoor activities, and adds emotional and financial pressure.

On cold winter days, average PM2.5 levels can reach as high as 600-700 µg/m³ in some districts — this is ~ 25-27 times higher than what the World Health Organization recommends as safe.

UB’s air pollution harms the environment by covering the city in smog, damaging plants and wildlife, and polluting soil and water.

The city’s valley geography traps the gas near the ground, making it stay longer in the air.
